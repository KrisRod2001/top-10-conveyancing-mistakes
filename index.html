Mistake #1: Signing the Contract Before Your Conveyancer Says “SAFE”

Average loss: $28,000 (full deposit)

Melbourne 2025: Sarah signed a $1.38M auction contract on Saturday. Monday, her conveyancer found an unregistered easement blocking her garage extension. Cooling-off had expired. She forfeited 10% deposit.
60-Second Fix: Forward every contract to your conveyancer before you sign. Insert clause: “Subject to conveyancer approval within 5 business days.”
2

Mistake #2: Trusting the Agent’s “Nothing to Worry About”

Average loss: $45,000 in blocked renovations

Section 32 statements often hide heritage overlays, flood zones, or contaminated land. Agents are not liable.

Fix: Order these 7 searches within 48 hours:
  1. Title Search
  2. Council Property Report
  3. Flood Overlay
  4. Bushfire Management Overlay
  5. Land Contamination (EPA)
  6. Heritage Victoria
  7. Planning Zones
3

Mistake #3: Accepting Estimated Rate Adjustments

Average overpayment: $7,200

Brisbane seller used 2024 council rates. Actual 2025 rates rose 18%. Buyer reclaimed $7,200 post-settlement via VCAT.
Fix: Demand final meter reads 3 days before settlement. Adjust to the cent.
4

Mistake #4: “The house looks fine” – Skipping Building & Pest

Average repair bill: $38,000

Termites, asbestos, and leaking roofs don’t show in photos.

Fix: Book a combined building + pest inspection on Day 2. Cost: $580. Saves tens of thousands.
5

Mistake #5: 14-Day Finance Clause in a 60-Day Market

Banks now take 21–28 days for unconditional approval.

Fix: Insert: “Subject to finance – 21 business days.”
6

Mistake #6: Ignoring PEXA Invite Until Settlement Week

Penalty interest: $180/day

PEXA requires digital verification 5 days early.

Fix: Click the PEXA email the day you receive it. Takes 90 seconds.
7

Mistake #7: Verbal Cooling-Off Cancellation

Must be written and delivered by 5 pm on day 3.

Fix: Email + registered post. Keep proof.
8

Mistake #8: Copy-Pasting Bank Details

One typo = funds to scammers.

Fix: Your conveyancer sends bank details via encrypted PEXA message. Never trust email.
9

Mistake #9: Buying a Unit Without Strata Report

Hidden special levies for lifts, fire upgrades, or cladding.

Fix: Order Body Corporate search Day 1 ($280). Reveals next 10 years of levies.

Mistake #10: DIY Conveyancing to “Save” $1,200

Average legal bill: $65,000

One missed caveat = years in court.

Fix: Hire a licensed conveyancer for $1,200. Sleep easy.
